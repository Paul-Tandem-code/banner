Identify carers
Recognise and respond to growing numbers of carers

There are more carers than ever. Drought, bushfire, flooding, economic insecurity and housing stress mean more people in our community are experiencing mental health challenges and distress – which means more people are finding themselves in caring roles for the first time.

The Government must work to identify communities where the need for carer support is growing and build targeted supports that meet the needs of those communities.

Support carers
Build both integrated and carer-specific services

Family, carers and supporters of people with mental health challenges need their own supports to address the full diversity of their needs, and not just only within the mental health system. The government must invest in ensuring all health and human services support mental health carers.

The government must extend funding for Victoria's first-of-a-kind Mental Health and Wellbeing Connect centres and establish new centres where they're most needed.

Involve carers
Include mental health carer voices and perspectives in system design and leadership

So much of mental health care is done by family, carers and supporters, who work hard in our communities to address gaps and harms, and drive positive change – often without compensation or recognition.

The government committed to putting the carer perspective and lived experience at the heart of our mental health and wellbeing system, and this commitment must be honoured.
